Introduction to LEXI Toys

Idea Behind the Model

Lexi Toys delivers educational toy kits to schools, designed to align with a custom curriculum that I’ve created, promoting lessons for children aged 4 – 7.

The model aims to spark interest in learning by introducing toys that match upcoming classroom topics, with subscriptions providing kits tailored to semester themes (e.g., science, puzzles).

The focus is on scalable growth, starting with small groups and expanding based on demand and financial viability.

Why Selling Toys Directly Instead of Calling It Teaching

Selling toys directly frames Lexi Toys as a product-based service, avoiding the need for educational licenses or teacher certifications, which are required for formal teaching programs.

This approach allows flexible pricing (e.g., 688 CNY per child per semester) and sidesteps government regulations on educational content, positioning it as a commercial enrichment tool.

The service provides toy kits and trainer visits as promotional support, not instruction, keeping it outside strict educational oversight.

Workarounds for Government Rules

Lexi Toys operates under a commercial business license, marketing toy subscriptions and trainer visits as enrichment services, not educational programs.

School partnerships are structured as vendor contracts, where schools opt into subscriptions, complying with procurement regulations rather than educational standards.

Trainers are engaged as independent contractors or staff under service agreements, ensuring they are not classified as educators, with activities limited to promotion to meet legal boundaries.

What a Trainer Does in the Classroom

The trainer visits schools (13 times per semester, about 20 minutes each) to introduce Lexi Toys kits that match the curriculum, promoting lessons teachers will later teach.

They create an encouraging atmosphere with activities like singing a song or playing a game, distributing toys to motivate kids, who use them at home or school to reinforce learning.

Trainers do not teach or explain lessons, serving only as a friendly face to inspire interest, while teachers handle all instruction, and trainers report session feedback to Lexi Toys.
